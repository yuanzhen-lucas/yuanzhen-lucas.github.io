About

I am a PhD student researching artificial intelligence for human disease. My work focuses on developing interpretable machine-learning models for clinical and biomedical data, including medical imaging and multi-omics datasets. I collaborate with clinicians to translate computational methods into tools that support diagnosis and treatment decisions.

Research

Artificial Intelligence and Human Disease

Developing AI and machine learning models to better understand and predict human diseases through biomedical data analysis.

Statistical Analysis of Public Health

Applying statistical modeling and data analysis techniques to identify patterns and determinants in large-scale health datasets.

Single-cell Technology Research

Exploring single-cell sequencing data to uncover cellular heterogeneity and mechanisms of disease progression.

Epigenetics in Cell Fate and Regeneration

Studying the epigenetic regulation of cell identity and tissue regeneration through integrative computational methods.

Projects

  • Project 1: AI-assisted Disease Diagnosis – Machine learning models for early detection of chronic diseases.
  • Project 2: Single-cell Transcriptomics Analysis – Identifying cell-type-specific biomarkers in regenerative biology.
